    Octopuses Have Three Hearts

    One heart pumps blood around the body, while the other two pump it to the gills. The blood is blue due to high copper levels.

    Reindeer Eyeballs Turn Blue in Winter

    Reindeer eyeballs turn blue in winter to help them see at lower light levels. (They're golden-colored in summer.) No other mammals are known to have this ability.

    Wild Dolphins Call Each Other by Name

    They let out a unique whistle to identify each other and will respond if they hear their own call played back.

    Male Gentoo and Adélie Penguins "Propose" with Pebbles

    Male gentoo and Adélie penguins "propose" to females by giving them a pebble. These are precious because the penguins use them to build their nests, and they can be hard to find along the barren Antarctic shoreline. If the female accepts the pebble, the pair bonds and mates for life.

    Spider Silk: Stronger Than Steel

    A single strand of spider silk is thinner than a human hair, but also five times stronger than steel of the same width. A rope just 2 inches thick could reportedly stop a Boeing 747.

    Platypus is the Only Animal That Has No Stomach

    The platypus, an egg-laying mammal native to Australia, is the only animal known to have no stomach. Instead, food goes directly from the esophagus to the intestines. This unique digestive system is adapted to the platypus's diet of insects, larvae, and small vertebrates.
